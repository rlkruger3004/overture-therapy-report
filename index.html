Key Findings

What the data is telling us — strengths, gaps, and opportunities

⚠️
Zero conversion tracking
Every row in the data shows 0.00 conversions. You're spending $630/month with no visibility into what's actually working. Setting up Google Ads conversion tracking for form fills and phone calls is the single most important action right now.
💸
Budget misaligned with niche
"Couples therapy" and "couples counseling" consume the most spend ($200+), yet "therapy for new moms" (8% CTR) and "emdr therapist westchester" (7.8% CTR) are your top performers. Budget should follow your niche, not generic keywords.
🔕
17 dormant keywords
Keywords like "support for mothers," "therapy for mom rage," "emotional support for moms," and "mom support" have never triggered a click. These are your exact audience — but they need dedicated ad copy and higher bids to compete.
✅
CTR is genuinely strong
4.22% CTR is approximately double the industry benchmark for therapy practices (~2%). When your ads do show, people are clicking. This means your ad copy is resonating — the opportunity is in showing up more often.
👩
Audience alignment is right
73% female, peak 35–44 age range — this matches the new and early motherhood demographic well. The campaign targeting is calibrated correctly. The challenge is reach and conversion, not relevance.
📱
Mobile is your primary channel
65% of spend and 66% of clicks come from mobile devices. Your landing page must be mobile-first — tap-to-call above the fold, fast load time, and a frictionless contact path are non-negotiable.
🔍
Untapped search intent
Real people in your area are searching "postpartum depression," "postpartum anxiety," "parental burnout," and "can't stop yelling at my kids" — and your ads appear but don't get clicked. Dedicated ad groups for these terms with matching copy would likely perform well.
📈
Only 16% impression share
You're only appearing in 1 of 6 eligible auctions. Raising daily budget or increasing bids on top-performing keywords could significantly increase reach without changing the campaign structure.

Conversion Strategy

How to turn ad clicks into inbound client relationships

The psychology of a therapy conversion

Someone searching for a therapist is typically experiencing high anxiety and high ambiguity. They're unsure if they need help, unsure if they're "bad enough" to deserve it, and scared of judgment. Every friction point — a long form, a vague CTA, a price they have to dig for — gives them a reason to close the tab and tell themselves they'll do it later. Your conversion mechanism has to reduce perceived risk and effort to near zero.

Rank 01 · Highest converting
Free 15-min consultation ★ recommended
The gold standard for therapy. It removes the biggest fear — committing to a stranger for a full hour. Framing matters enormously: "free 15-min call" converts better than "free consultation" because it sounds lighter and less clinical.
CTA to use: "Book your free call" — not "Schedule a consultation"
Rank 02 · Mobile-critical
Tap-to-call button
Your data shows 66% of ad traffic is on mobile. A tap-to-call button above the fold with a warm label like "Talk to us today" is one of the simplest highest-converting mechanisms for therapy. Many clients prefer calling when they're in an emotional moment.
Placement: Above the fold on mobile, before any other content
Rank 03 · Low friction
Short contact form (3 fields)
Name, email, and one open question: "What brings you here?" converts significantly better than long intake forms. The open question makes the client feel heard immediately and qualifies the lead for you without feeling like paperwork.
Never ask for insurance, phone, and symptoms before they've connected
Rank 04 · Nurture path
Lead magnet with email capture
A free downloadable resource in exchange for an email address. Works best when the content speaks directly to the pain point your audience is already searching for — postpartum anxiety, mom burnout, or starting therapy for the first time.
Best for: People not ready to book yet — captures them in a nurture sequence
Rank 05 · Personalization
"Match me with a therapist" quiz
A 3–5 question quiz ("What are you hoping to work on?" / "Do you prefer morning or evening sessions?") converts well because it feels personalized and low-commitment. The quiz completion IS the conversion event — the follow-up email becomes the nurture sequence.
Good for: Multi-therapist practices where fit matters
Rank 06 · Zero friction
Self-scheduling embedded on page
Tools like Calendly, SimplePractice, or Jane App let visitors book a slot without any human interaction. For people who feel shame about reaching out, removing the human gatekeeper from the first touchpoint dramatically increases conversion.
CTA to use: "See available times" — not "Request an appointment"
What kills conversions in therapy specifically
Vague CTAs"Learn more" or "Contact us" perform poorly. Be specific: "Book a free 15-min call"
Long intake forms upfrontSave the 30-field intake for after they've booked — not before
Pricing too prominentPricing should be findable but not the first thing a first-time visitor sees
Stock photos of sadnessPeople crying or lying on couches increase anxiety rather than safety
Slow mobile load timeYour audience is on mobile at night, and patience is very low
No social proof near CTAOne testimonial or credential note next to the booking button meaningfully lifts conversion

Priority Action Plan

Ranked next steps for Overture Therapy's social media and marketing team

Set up conversion tracking immediately Install Google Ads conversion tags for form fills, phone calls, and Calendly bookings. Without this, no optimization is possible and budget decisions are made blind.
Add tap-to-call above the fold on mobile landing page 65% of your traffic is mobile. A warm, labeled call button (e.g. "Talk to us today") is the single highest-ROI landing page change you can make right now.
Shift budget toward niche motherhood keywords "Therapy for new moms" (8% CTR) and "emdr therapist westchester" (7.8%) are outperforming everything. Increase bids and pause the zero-click keywords to fund them.
Create dedicated ad groups for postpartum & parental burnout Real people in Westchester are searching "postpartum anxiety," "parental burnout," "can't stop yelling at my kids." Write ad copy that speaks directly to those pain points — not generic therapy copy.
Build one lead magnet for mothers not ready to book A free PDF guide — "The Postpartum Anxiety Guide: What's Normal, What's Not, and When to Get Help" — captures email addresses and starts a nurture relationship with the 80% of visitors who won't book on first visit.
Increase bids during 9am–3pm Monday–Wednesday Your data shows peak impression volume during these windows. Use bid adjustments (+20–30%) to increase presence when your audience is most active and most likely to be in a decision-making mindset.
Embed Calendly or SimplePractice for zero-friction self-scheduling Replace any "contact us" form with a live availability calendar. This removes the human gatekeeper from the first touchpoint, which meaningfully lifts conversion especially for people experiencing shame or hesitation.
